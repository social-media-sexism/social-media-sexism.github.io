Twitter

In today’s society, many individuals are always on their phones either on their social media accounts or playing a game. If you go on a train, you see a teenager on instagram. If you go on the bus, you see a twenty year old man on their snapchat. If you go to dinner with your family, you see your aunts and uncles on facebook. Absolutely everyone has some sort of social media account online where they share their personal information with people that they either know or do not know. To many, social media is a way to escape from reality and be a different person behind a screen. However, even though to some people social media is considered a blessing, to other people it is the exact opposite. Social media can serve as a platform for people to express inappropriate thoughts on topics and bully online accounts.

Twitter is a prime example of a social media platform where many people share their thoughts. Twitter was created in March 2006 by Jack Dorsey and the main purpose of the application was to listen and be aware of social media conservations. Many people can tweet instantly about whatever they wish at any given time. There are millions of people who have twitter accounts and use the app as a way to self express. However people use twitter to attack the other gender. Many men and women post sexist comments on twitter about a person's appearance and the way they act. Yet, a majority of tweets attack women. Countless of the tweets degrade women and insult them with misogynistic remarks. Because social media is filled with images of thin and beautiful women, many women are expected to look like that in society, creating an unrealistic expectation of a women’s body. Furthermore, women are judged by the way they speak or act on a normal basis. As a result, according to “Facebook and Twitter ‘harm young people’s mental health’”, the article states that “platforms have a negative effect because they can exacerbate children’s and young people’s body image worries, and worsen bullying, sleep problems and feelings of anxiety, depression and loneliness”. Overall Twitter and any other social media platform heavily affects a person's mental health with many misogynistic and negative comments. In conclusion, sexism is clearly evident everywhere in modern day social media. Women are constantly objectified by the way they look and are constantly called a “bitch” or any other misogynistic term. Social media platforms can really cause a toll on a woman's life and really impact their mental issues and self esteem issues. However, as a women, we should not give up on equality but instead fight for the equality we deserve so we can end sexism not only in social media, but in our everyday lives.
